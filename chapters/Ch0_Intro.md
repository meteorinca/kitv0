# Vocabulary to Know

* **Electromagnetism:** A fundamental force of nature that describes how electrically charged particles interact with one another and with magnetic fields, serving as the physical foundation for generating power, transmitting wireless signals, and moving data in modern tech.
* **Computer:** A machine that takes in information, manipulates it according to a specific set of rules or instructions (an algorithm), and produces a result. It is defined by its logical function—processing data—whether that processing is done by gears, flowing water, or electricity.
* **Electronic Computer:** A specific type of computer that uses the flow of electrical currents and the manipulation of magnetic fields to represent, store, and process information at incredibly high speeds.
* **Microcontroller:** A self-contained, miniature electronic computer shrunk down onto a single microchip, specifically designed to dedicatedly govern a single task or device—like managing the temperature in a microwave or controlling the braking system in a car.
* **Programming:** The act of translating human logic and problem-solving into a precise, step-by-step sequence of instructions that a computer can understand and execute to achieve a desired outcome.
* **Large Language Models (LLMs):** A type of artificial intelligence software that processes vast amounts of human text to map out the statistical relationships between words, enabling it to recognize, predict, and generate human-like language in response to a prompt.
* **Algorithm:** A precise, step-by-step set of instructions or rules designed to solve a specific problem or perform a task. It is the conceptual blueprint for a process, completely independent of the language or machine used to execute it.
* **Binary:** A system of representing information using only two distinct states—typically denoted as 0 and 1. Computers use binary because physical mediums are highly susceptible to "noise" (interference, voltage drops, or wear); distinguishing between just two extreme states (like "electricity fully on" vs. "electricity completely off," or "a gear turned left" vs. "turned right") is incredibly reliable and prevents errors.
* **Machine:** A physical apparatus made up of interrelated parts that takes in an input of energy (whether mechanical, electrical, thermal, or human) and directs it to perform a specific type of work or objective.
* **Robot:** A machine capable of carrying out a complex series of actions automatically. Unlike a simple machine, a robot is defined by a feedback loop: it uses sensors to perceive its environment, a computer to process that information and make decisions, and actuators to physically act upon the world.
